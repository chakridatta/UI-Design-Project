Alcohol-Related Liver Damage

Understand the consequences of alcohol on liver health

Overview

Alcohol-related liver disease (ARLD) includes three conditions—fatty liver, alcoholic hepatitis, and cirrhosis—caused by excessive alcohol consumption. It’s one of the leading causes of preventable liver damage worldwide.

Key Facts

  • Fatty liver is reversible with abstinence.
  • Alcoholic hepatitis can be life-threatening.
  • Cirrhosis is permanent damage but progression can be slowed.

Three Stages of Damage

Fatty Liver

Early stage where fat builds up in liver cells. Usually no symptoms. Reversible with alcohol cessation.

Alcoholic Hepatitis

Inflammation of the liver. Can cause jaundice, fever, nausea, and abdominal pain. Requires immediate medical attention.

Cirrhosis

Scarring and permanent damage. May lead to liver failure, confusion, internal bleeding. Requires lifelong management or transplant.

Protect Your Liver

Limit Intake

Stay within recommended alcohol limits. Avoid binge drinking.

Hydrate

Drink plenty of water to support detoxification and liver function.

Regular Checkups

Monitor liver enzymes and seek help at first sign of fatigue, jaundice, or pain.
