Hepatitis Awareness

Understand the types, causes, prevention, and treatment of Hepatitis

What is Hepatitis?

Hepatitis refers to inflammation of the liver, usually caused by viral infections, but also due to toxins, alcohol use, certain medications, and autoimmune diseases. It can range from a mild illness lasting a few weeks to a serious, lifelong condition.

Explore Major Types

Hepatitis A (HAV)

This form is usually short-term and spread through ingestion of contaminated food or water. It’s preventable with a vaccine.

  • Transmission: Fecal-oral route
  • Symptoms: Fever, fatigue, nausea, jaundice
  • Prevention: Hygiene, clean water, vaccination

Hepatitis B (HBV)

This virus is spread through blood, semen, or other bodily fluids. It can become chronic and lead to serious complications like cirrhosis or liver cancer.

  • Transmission: Sexual contact, needles, childbirth
  • Symptoms: Similar to HAV but may be more severe
  • Prevention: Vaccination, safe practices

Hepatitis C (HCV)

Mostly spread through contact with infected blood, it often leads to chronic infection and liver damage. No vaccine is currently available, but it's curable with medication.

  • Transmission: Needles, blood transfusion
  • Symptoms: Often silent until liver damage occurs
  • Prevention: Safe needle use, blood screening

Other Types

Hepatitis D (HDV)

This only occurs in those already infected with Hepatitis B. It’s rare but leads to more severe outcomes.

Hepatitis E (HEV)

Spread through contaminated water, mainly in developing countries. It’s usually acute and self-limiting.

When to See a Doctor

If you notice signs like persistent fatigue, yellowing of the skin or eyes, abdominal pain, or dark urine, it's important to consult a healthcare provider for diagnosis and treatment.

Prevention Tips

  • Get vaccinated for Hepatitis A and B
  • Practice safe hygiene and food handling
  • Avoid sharing needles or personal items
  • Use protection during sex
  • Get tested regularly if you're at risk
