Empowering You to Manage Diabetes

Evidence-based information to help you understand and control your diabetes

Understanding Diabetes

Diabetes is a chronic metabolic disorder characterized by elevated levels of blood glucose (blood sugar) that leads over time to serious damage to the heart, blood vessels, eyes, kidneys, and nerves.

Diabetes Facts

According to the World Health Organization, about 422 million people worldwide have diabetes, particularly in low-and middle-income countries, and 1.5 million deaths are directly attributed to diabetes each year.

Types of Diabetes

Type Description Prevalence
Type 1 Diabetes Autoimmune condition where the pancreas produces little or no insulin. Requires daily insulin administration. 5-10% of cases
Type 2 Diabetes Body becomes resistant to insulin or doesn't produce enough. Often related to lifestyle factors. 90-95% of cases
Gestational Diabetes Develops during pregnancy and usually disappears after giving birth. Increases risk of type 2 diabetes later. 2-10% of pregnancies

Diabetes Warning Signs

  • Increased thirst and frequent urination
  • Extreme hunger and fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
  • Unexplained weight loss (Type 1)

Prevention & Management

Strategy Recommendation Benefits
Healthy Eating Focus on vegetables, whole grains, lean protein. Limit processed foods and sugary drinks. Better blood sugar control, weight management
Physical Activity 150 minutes moderate exercise weekly (e.g., brisk walking) Improves insulin sensitivity, lowers blood sugar
Weight Management Lose 5-10% of body weight if overweight Reduces diabetes risk by 58%
Regular Monitoring Check blood sugar as recommended by your doctor Early detection of problems, better control
Stress Reduction Practice mindfulness, meditation, or yoga Lowers stress hormones that raise blood sugar
