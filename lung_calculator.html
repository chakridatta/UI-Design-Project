Your Lung Health Assessment

Understanding FEV1

FEV1 measures how much air you can forcefully exhale in one second. It helps diagnose conditions like asthma, COPD, and pulmonary fibrosis. Lower than normal values may indicate restricted airways.

Normal ranges vary by age, gender, and height. This tool calculates your predicted FEV1 based on these factors.

Improving Lung Health

  • Avoid smoking: The #1 way to protect your lungs
  • Exercise regularly: Strengthens respiratory muscles
  • Reduce pollutants: Avoid secondhand smoke and outdoor air pollution when possible
  • Deep breathing: Practice diaphragmatic breathing exercises
  • Stay hydrated: Helps keep mucosal linings thin
  • Prevent infections: Wash hands regularly and get recommended vaccines

When to See a Doctor

Consult a healthcare professional if you experience:

  • Shortness of breath during routine activities
  • Chronic cough lasting more than 3 weeks
  • Wheezing or chest tightness
  • Coughing up blood
  • Frequent respiratory infections
  • Unexplained weight loss with breathing issues

Early detection of lung problems leads to better outcomes.
